Metal Tier System


Fronstrum - The hardest known metal in existance, not even the gods know how to forge it. But, certain cosmic beings and consciousnesses are said to know the secrets of forging it. 100% more expensive to enchant.

Weapons: +5 to attack and damage. Ignore any AC for armor that is not fronstrum.

Armor: +4 AC. Resistance to all damage that is not from a fronstrum weapon. Immunity to critical hits.

Coloration - No real color, but still can be seen.


Uru - The favored material of the gods themselves along with their chosen warriors and angels, uru is the most celestial of all metals. 50% cheaper to enchant. Requires the energy of a god or star to forge.

Weapons: +2 to attack and damage. Attacks deal an additional 2d8 radiant or necrotic damage. Ignores AC bonus of non-magical armor.

Armor: +2 AC. Resistance to radiant and necrotic damage. Resistance to non-magical attacks.

Coloration - Deep silver with veins of black or gold.


Adamantine - 10% cheaper to enchant.

Weapons: +1 to attack and damage.

Armor: +1 AC and immunity to critical hits.

Coloration - Black, very reflective.


Dark Metal - 25% cheaper to enchant. Can store up to a third level spell, divided however the user wishes.

Weapons: +1 to damage.

Coloration - Near absence of light.


Mythril - Weighs 1/2 its weight, 5% cheaper to enchant.

Coloration - Blueish-grey.


Steel - Normal assumed properties.

Coloration - Light to dark grey.


Iron -

Weapons: Piercing and slashing weapons need to sharpen after every 4 combats, or damage die reduces by one stage and becomes bludgeoning. Dark metal and up armor pieces gain +1 to AC against iron.

Armor: AC -1 if attacked by dark metal and up.

Coloration - Light to dark grey.


Bronze -

Weapons: Piercing and slashing weapons need to sharpen after every 2 combats, or damage die reduces by one stage and becomes bludgeoning. Steel and up armor pieces gain +1 to AC against Bronze.

Armor: AC -1 if attacked by steel and up.

Coloration - Dark brown.


Copper -

Weapons: Piercing and slashing weapons need to sharpen after every 4 combats, or damage die reduces by one stage and becomes bludgeoning. Iron and up armor pieces gain +1 to AC against copper.

Armor: AC -1 if attacked by iron and up.

Coloration - Light brown.


Obsidian -

Weapons: On a natural one, has 50% chance to shatter.

Armor: Shatters upon crit against it.

Coloration - Glasslike black.


Ceramic, Bone, Flint -

Weapons: On a natural 1 or 2, has 75% chance to shatter.

Armor: Impossible.


Special

Celestial Bronze, Imperial Gold - Mined directly from the planes of the celestial gods, this material is devestating against magical creatures, but cannot come into contact with non-magical ones.

Weapons: +2 to attack and damage, damage die increases by 2 stages. Non-magical creatures cannot be damaged.

Armor: +2 to AC, resistance to radiant and necrotic damage. Non-magical attacks ignore the AC bonus of this armor.


Necrodermis - Pulled from the shadowfell, this material heals itself extremely effectively, along with the user. It is highly resistant to the energies from the Shadowfell.

Weapons: +1 to attack and damage. Deal an additional 2d6 necrotic damage. Heal for necrotic damage dealt. Only works on living creatures. Upon killing a foe, gain 4d4 temporary hit points.

Armor: Resistant to necrotic and cold damage, heal 1d6 per 4 hit die of user every short rest.
